The Principal Investigator of SmartScape Design Lab, Dr. Jinda Qi, has received the 2026 NParks grant as Co-Principal Investigator for a project on human-centric urban thermal comfort perception, greenery, and LiDAR-based environmental measurement in dense cityscapes. The project adopts a human-centric approach to examine how urban greenery influences thermal comfort perception across different land use types, urban forms, and socio-demographic groups in Singapore. 

Running from 2026 to 2029, the project will identify effective greenery-based design interventions for high-density urban environments. It will also develop decision-support guidelines and a digital platform to help stakeholders optimize greenery strategies for improving thermal comfort at local and microscale levels.
